# Polygonal-Mesh Reproduction of the AMG Threshold-Tuning Experiments

**Project:** Extending AMG-DL (arXiv:2111.01629) from FEM to polytopic Discontinuous Galerkin meshes
**Date:** 2026-07-08
**Solver backend:** pyamg 5.x classical Ruge–Stüben (paper used HYPRE BoomerAMG)

---

## 1. Objective

Reproduce the paper's diffusion numerical experiments — the relationships between the
AMG strong-threshold `theta`, the convergence factor `rho`, solve cost, and hierarchy
depth — but on **agglomerated polytopic DG matrices** instead of the paper's structured
FEM meshes. Success means the same qualitative trends appear, confirming that the
ML-tuning premise transfers to the polygonal setting.

## 2. Pipeline

```
hyper_cube[-1,1]^2  --refine 7-->  16384 quad cells
      |  METIS agglomeration (GridTools::partition_triangulation)
      v
n_subdomains polytopes  --weighted SIP-DG (Ern-Stephansen-Zunino)-->  SPD matrix A
      |  polydg_diffusion_hetero.cc  ->  .mtx + geometry.csv
      v
pyamg Ruge-Stuben + CG  ->  rho, iterations, elapsed_sec, n_levels
      |  generate_report.py
      v
D.csv (14 columns)  ->  AMG-DL/scripts/*.py  ->  figures
```

The matrix is assembled with a **coefficient-weighted SIP-DG** scheme: per-polytope
constant `mu_I = mu(bbox_center)`, harmonic face weighting
`mu_w = mu_I*mu_J / (mu_I + mu_J)`, penalty `2*mu_w / h`. This is provably coercive at
high contrast and reduces to standard SIP-DG when `mu_I = mu_J`. It was validated to be
symmetric positive-definite at every contrast level tested (min eigenvalue > 0).

## 3. Experiment grid

| Axis | Values | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Coefficient pattern | vertical_stripes, checkerboard_2x2, vertical_stripes_4, checkerboard_4x4 | the paper's 4 structured patterns on [-1,1]^2, high = 10^epsilon, low = 1 |
| Contrast `epsilon` | 0.0, 0.8, 1.6, 2.4, 5.0 | high coefficient = 10^epsilon (epsilon=9.5 excluded, see §6) |
| Problem size (`n_subdomains`) | 150, 250, 400, 600 | polytope count = size axis (see §5) |
| `theta` | 10 points, linspace(0.02, 0.9) | AMG strong threshold |

**Size axis (one distinct `h` per size):**

| n_subdomains | h (max polytope diameter) | matrix n | nnz |
|---|---|---|---|
| 150 | 0.3511 | 600 | 15296 |
| 250 | 0.2764 | 1000 | 25824 |
| 400 | 0.2176 | 1600 | 41952 |
| 600 | 0.1907 | 2400 | 63680 |

Total: 4 patterns × 5 epsilons × 4 sizes × 10 thetas = **800 rows** in D.csv.

## 4. Results

Four figure families were regenerated by `AMG-DL/scripts/run_figure_pipeline.sh`, all
under `results_poly/figures/`.

### 4.1 theta–rho relation (`theta_rho_relation/`)

30 tables (`<pattern>_theta_<value>.csv`) + LaTeX. Each cell is `rho(iterations)` over
the epsilon × h grid. Two robust trends:

- **rho decreases monotonically with theta.** Example (checkerboard_4x4, h=0.191):
  rho = 0.876 at theta=0.02 → 0.816 at theta=0.9.
- **rho increases as the mesh refines** (h ↓, larger system). Example
  (vertical_stripes, theta=0.02, epsilon=0): rho = 0.777 → 0.807 → 0.848 → 0.876
  as n_subdomains goes 150 → 250 → 400 → 600.

### 4.2 theta–cost plots (`theta_cost_relation/`)

One PNG + summary CSV per pattern (mean elapsed_sec vs theta). Cost tracks rho: higher
theta → fewer iterations → less time, mirroring the paper's "rho and solve time are
strongly correlated" claim.

### 4.3 rho–time scatter (`rho_time_scatter/`)

Correlation over all 800 rows:

| pair | Pearson r |
|------|-----------|
| rho vs iterations | **0.967** |
| iterations vs elapsed_sec | 0.693 |
| rho vs elapsed_sec | 0.678 |

The near-perfect rho–iterations correlation confirms rho is a faithful cost proxy. The
weaker elapsed-time correlations are expected: pyamg's Python timing is noisy on these
small matrices (this is a known limitation, not a data issue).

### 4.4 theta vs hierarchy depth (`theta_nlevels/`)

`n_levels` spans 4–6 across the grid. Higher theta (more aggressive coarsening) tends to
add levels. 80 per-test-case regressions of normalized-levels vs theta were fit; results
in `regression_stats.csv`.

## 5. Key finding — polygonal meshes prefer a *high* theta

At the finest mesh (h=0.191), the convergence-optimal theta is **0.9 for every
(pattern, epsilon)** — the top of the swept range. Compared against the common FEM
default theta≈0.25:

| pattern | epsilon | best theta | rho(best) | rho(theta=0.25) | improvement |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| checkerboard_4x4 | 0.0 | 0.90 | 0.816 | 0.861 | 5.2% |
| checkerboard_4x4 | 5.0 | 0.90 | 0.819 | 0.877 | 6.5% |
| vertical_stripes_4 | 2.4 | 0.90 | 0.796 | 0.862 | 7.7% |

This is the central thesis result reproduced in the polygonal setting: **the DG/polytopic
operator wants a different (much higher) strong threshold than the FEM default**, so a
tuned/learned theta yields a real convergence gain. It is exactly the signal the Stage-1
GNN is meant to predict.

> Note: at coarser meshes and higher contrast the argmin theta is less saturated (earlier
> probes at n_subdomains=400 showed the optimum shifting between 0.75 and 0.9 as contrast
> changed, with theta=0.9 rebounding at high epsilon). The full-grid optimum lands at 0.9
> because the swept upper bound is 0.9; widening the theta grid past 0.9 is a possible
> refinement.

## 6. Caveats (documented honestly)

1. **epsilon capped at 5.0.** At epsilon=9.5 (contrast 10^9.5, condition number ~10^10–10^11),
   pyamg's classical Ruge–Stüben + double precision breaks down: CG either stalls at the
   1000-iteration cap or exits early with `Inner denominator was zero.`, producing
   non-physical rho (including rho ≥ 1, impossible for SPD-preconditioned CG). Those rows
   were filtered out. epsilon ≤ 5.0 rows are all clean (rho ∈ [0.669, 0.889], iterations
   ∈ [69, 238], zero rho ≥ 1). The paper's HYPRE BoomerAMG is more robust at extreme
   contrast; matching epsilon=9.5 would require that backend.

2. **Absolute values are on pyamg's scale**, not bit-comparable to the paper's HYPRE FEM
   numbers. The theta-trends, the rho–iterations correlation, and the high-theta optimum
   all carry over; the absolute rho/timing magnitudes do not.

3. **Mesh type.** Polytopes are METIS agglomerations of a uniform quad grid — rectilinear
   polygons with variable (8–16) right-angle corners, not Voronoi cells and not a fixed
   corner count.

## 7. Reproduction

```bash
# 1. generate D.csv (on the cluster, in the Polydeal build venv)
python3 polydeal_driver/generate_report.py \
  --driver ./examples/polydg_diffusion_hetero.g \
  --out-root results_poly/datasets/diffusion/large \
  --scale large --n-subdomains 150 250 400 600 \
  --patterns 0 1 2 3 --epsilons 0 0.8 1.6 2.4 5.0 --refinement 7 --n-thetas 10

# 2. render figures (locally; AMG-DL is a local subfolder)
export PYTHONPATH=$PWD/AMG-DL
python3 AMG-DL/scripts/generate_theta_rho_tables.py \
  --input_glob "datasets/diffusion/large/**/raw/diffusion_reports/*.csv" \
  --out_dir results_poly/figures/theta_rho_relation
python3 AMG-DL/scripts/generate_theta_cost_plots.py \
  --input-glob "datasets/diffusion/large/**/raw/diffusion_reports/*.csv" \
  --out-dir results_poly/figures/theta_cost_relation \
  --patterns vertical_stripes,checkerboard_2x2,vertical_stripes_4,checkerboard_4x4 \
  --epsilons 0.0,0.8,1.6,2.4,5.0 \
  --h-values 0.19072743,0.21763107,0.27643447,0.3511282 --png
python3 AMG-DL/scripts/generate_rho_time_scatter.py \
  --input-glob "datasets/diffusion/large/**/raw/diffusion_reports/*.csv" \
  --out-dir results_poly/figures/rho_time_scatter \
  --h-values 0.19072743,0.21763107,0.27643447,0.3511282
python3 AMG-DL/scripts/theta_vs_nlevels_plot.py \
  --dataset-root datasets/diffusion/large \
  --output-dir results_poly/figures/theta_nlevels --include-splits all
```

**Note on figure-script filters:** `generate_theta_cost_plots.py` and
`generate_rho_time_scatter.py` default their `--h-values` to the paper's FEM mesh sizes.
Those must be overridden with the polygonal `h` values (above) or every row is filtered
out.
